About the Founder

My first job in language education had no title. I'm the son of a single immigrant mother, and I grew up learning English and Spanish at home while teaching her English: translating at appointments, decoding paperwork, serving as her cultural broker long before I had a word for it. I saw up close what it costs when language stands between a person and full participation in their own life. The Language Blueprint exists for kids who were like my mom.

Educator

I teach secondary multilingual learners in New York: newcomers and Students with Limited or Interrupted Formal Education (SLIFE), the students most schools are least prepared to serve. My classroom is where second language acquisition theory meets a bell schedule. I design sheltered instruction that gives students at the earliest stages of English real access to grade-level texts and ideas, along with a genuine pathway to the NYS English Regents and graduation. And because what students read matters as much as how, I build curriculum around culturally and linguistically sustaining literature, so my students meet their own languages, communities, and experiences on the page.

Researcher

My research in applied linguistics and literacy education starts from an uncomfortable observation: the field's dominant theories of reading, vocabulary, and assessment quietly assume a print-literate learner, an assumption SLIFE students specifically do not share. I'm working through what vocabulary development, reading comprehension, and valid assessment look like when that assumption is removed. At the center of this work is a reconceptualization of reading comprehension for SLIFE, and a clear account of what that reconceptualization needs to translate to in technology designed for multilingual learners who are SLIFE. The same lens shapes my daily practice, where I design construct-valid classroom assessments and challenge the measures that put construct-irrelevant barriers in front of multilingual learners and students with disabilities.

Builder

I designed The Reading Blueprint, an AI-powered reading tool built from the ground up for learners without assumed print literacy, the users most edtech quietly designs out. It pairs multimodal scaffolds with vocabulary supports mapped directly to acquisition theory, and treats students' full linguistic repertoires as assets rather than interference. It's a classroom tool and a research instrument in one, with formal validation studies of its effects on SLIFE students' reading comprehension anticipated in the future.

The Through-Line

The students hardest to serve with off-the-shelf tools deserve the best-designed ones. That conviction connects my classroom, my research, and my code. It's why I founded The Language Blueprint LLC, and it all comes back to equity of access: to literacy, to language, and to the futures both make possible.
