About Us

Anthony J. Guzmán

Founder, The Language Blueprint LLC

I'm an English as a New Language (ENL) and ELA educator, applied linguistics researcher, and education technology designer working at the intersection of language, literacy, and AI. My work centers on one question: how do we design instruction and tools that actually serve multilingual learners and any student who struggles with language, reading, or literacy, especially those the field too often overlooks?

Educator First

I teach secondary multilingual learners in New York, including newcomers and Students with Limited or Interrupted Formal Education (SLIFE). My classroom practice is grounded in second language acquisition theory and shaped by frameworks I use daily: comprehensible input and pushed output, translanguaging, task-based language teaching, Universal Design for Learning, and differentiated instruction. I design sheltered instruction that gives students at the Entering and Emerging stages of English proficiency genuine access to grade-level texts and ideas, and a pathway to graduation milestones like the NYS English Regents.

Just as important to me is what students read. I build curriculum around culturally and linguistically sustaining literature, choosing texts in which my students can see their own languages, communities, and experiences reflected.

Researcher and Writer

As a graduate researcher in TESOL and Applied Linguistics, I study L2 vocabulary acquisition, reading comprehension, and language assessment. My dissertation uses The Reading Blueprint (SLIFE Reading Assistant), the AI-mediated reading tool I designed and built, as its research instrument. It operationalizes second language acquisition and TESOL constructs, WIDA-referenced comprehensible input, multidimensional and learning-oriented assessment, MALP-grounded simultaneous literacy, language, and content development, and translanguaging as interface design, into a working system. That lets me study how AI-mediated, culturally responsive scaffolding affects SLIFE students' reading comprehension development, and examine the validity of the tool's adaptive, WIDA-aligned assessment mechanism. I'm developing this work for publication in peer-reviewed venues in applied linguistics and literacy education.

I also bring an assessment lens to everything I do, from designing construct-valid classroom assessments to critiquing where standard measures introduce construct-irrelevant barriers for multilingual learners and students with disabilities.

Builder

The Reading Blueprint is an AI-mediated reading support and assessment tool built for Students with Limited or Interrupted Formal Education (SLIFE). It simplifies classroom texts to a student's WIDA proficiency level while preserving, rather than erasing, culturally specific references, and provides bilingual vocabulary and cultural-context scaffolding on demand. A task-based conversational practice agent lets students talk through the text they just read, and a five-dimension comprehension assessment, covering literal recall, inference, vocabulary in context, main idea, and personal connection, adaptively updates the student's WIDA level as they progress. A companion teacher dashboard provides class-wide progress monitoring, per-student performance visualizations, and AI-drafted, teacher-reviewed micro-intervention plans.

Under the hood, it's a Python/FastAPI backend with an unframeworked HTML/CSS/JavaScript frontend across three interfaces: a student app, a teacher dashboard, and a standalone assessment view. Text adaptation, assessment generation, and cultural explanations are produced by Anthropic's Claude; illustrative images are generated via Google Cloud Vertex AI's Imagen 3, with a Wikipedia/Wikimedia fallback. Student and teacher accounts, session history, WIDA progression, vocabulary and cultural-term lookups, and assigned assessments are stored in a PostgreSQL database on Google Cloud SQL. The application is containerized with Docker, hosted on Google Cloud Run, and deployed continuously through GitHub Actions.
